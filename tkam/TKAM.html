Education in To Kill a Mockingbird

"Everybody is a genius. But if you judge a fish by it's ability to climb a tree, it will live it's whole life believing that it is stupid" (Albert Einstein)


In To Kill a Mockingbird, Harper Lee shows how standardized education while efficient, is holding people and society back.

I chose this claim because Harper Lee touches on the flaws of education, but it is also one of the more under represented things talked about when To Kill a Mockingbird is discussed.



In this graph, we can see how more and more people are literate throughout history. This, while certainly a good thing, leads to an almost mass production assembly line of education, where many corners can be cut.
