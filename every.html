Hi. What you see above is a little project I made in study hall. It might just look like random static, and, well, that is what it is, but there's more to it than that.

You see, it truly is RANDOM static. Every pixel of the 100x50 screen is given a random color, for every frame. Essentially, each frame is just a randomly generated image. So most of the time, it will look like colorful static. Most of the time.

Because of it's inherent randomness, there is a chance, while small, for literally anything to be generated. Any combination of pixels on the screen have a chance of showing up.

Pictures of space, cats, classified documents, and the Hoover Dam are all possible. This could even included a frame from, for example, Star Wars.

If a frame from a video can generate, then, though it is an astronomically small chance, the next frame could also generate the following frame. Then the next, and the next, and so on.

This means that not only can any picture be generated, but also any video.

Of course, the chance of this happening is insanely small, but if you watched it long enough, say an infinite amount of time, you would see everything.

Every movie that has ever come out. Every movie that hasn't come out. Every movie that will ever come out.

Historical battles with 100% accuracy.

Entire lives, from the person's perspective, birth to death. In fact, Every life. From other angles as well.

You're not looking at random static. You're looking at a time machine. You're looking at the universe. You're looking at everything.
